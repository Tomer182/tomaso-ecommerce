## The Three-Zone System That Actually Works

Most WFH advice focuses on gear. But before you buy anything, you need to understand zones.

### Zone 1: Deep Work Zone
**Purpose:** Focused, uninterrupted concentration  
**What you need:** Desk, good chair, monitor(s), minimal distractions  
**My setup:** Corner desk, ergonomic chair, 27" monitor, noise-cancelling headphones, desk lamp

**The rule:** When you're in this zone, you're WORKING working. No phone. No TV. No "quick browse" of social media.

### Zone 2: Communication Zone
**Purpose:** Calls, meetings, emails  
**What you need:** Good lighting, clean background, quality microphone  
**My setup:** Same desk, but angled toward window for natural light, small ring light for cloudy days, USB microphone

**The rule:** This is where you're visible to others. Keep it presentable (but not obsessively so‚Äîwe're at home, not a TV studio).

### Zone 3: Break Zone
**Purpose:** Actual breaks where you're NOT working  
**What you need:** Anywhere that's NOT your work zone  
**My setup:** Couch, kitchen, balcony‚Äîanywhere I can physically leave the work space

**The rule (most important one):** Never eat lunch at your desk. Never "just check one thing" during a break. Physical separation = mental separation.

## The Setup: Step-by-Step

You've got gear. Now let's set it up correctly. Most people skip this and wonder why their fancy chair still hurts.

### Desk Height
1. **Sit normally**
2. **Elbows at 90¬∞ when typing** - that's your desk height
3. **Too high?** Lower chair or get a keyboard tray
4. **Too low?** Raise desk (books under legs work) or get a lower chair

### Monitor Height
1. **Top of screen at or slightly below eye level**
2. **About arm's length away**
3. **Use books/stand/riser to adjust** - do NOT hunch forward

### Chair Position
1. **Feet flat on floor** (or footrest if you're short)
2. **Thighs parallel to ground** (90¬∞ at knees)
3. **Lumbar support touching your lower back**
4. **Armrests at height where shoulders are relaxed** (not hunched up or sagging down)

### Lighting
1. **Natural light from the side** (not behind you‚Äîcauses backlight glare on camera)
2. **Desk lamp for task lighting**
3. **No overhead lights directly above monitor** (glare)

**Time investment:** 15 minutes to dial this in  
**Impact:** Instant comfort improvement

## The Daily Routine That Keeps You Sane

Having the right setup is half the battle. The other half is routine.

### My Day (After 5 Years of Trial and Error)

**7:00 AM** - Wake up, normal morning routine  
**8:00 AM** - "Commute" (walk around the block for 10 min) - sounds dumb, works amazingly  
**8:15 AM** - Start work, Deep Work Zone, phone in other room  
**10:00 AM** - 10-min break, walk to kitchen, snack, back  
**10:10 AM** - Deep work continues  
**12:00 PM** - Lunch AWAY from desk (non-negotiable)  
**12:45 PM** - Light walk (even just 5 min)  
**1:00 PM** - Meetings/emails/communication work  
**3:00 PM** - 10-min break  
**3:10 PM** - Final deep work block  
**5:00 PM** - STOP. Shut laptop. Leave Zone 1.  
**5:10 PM** - "Commute" home (another walk around the block)  
**5:20 PM** - Actually off work

**Why this works:**
- Clear start and end (no endless work blur)
- Movement breaks (sitting is killing you)
- Deep work when energy is high (morning)
- Communication work when energy is lower (afternoon)
- Physical separation (Zone system)

## The Boundaries Nobody Talks About

The hardest part of WFH isn't the setup‚Äîit's the boundaries.

### Boundary 1: Work Hours
**The problem:** Work never ends when your office is your home

**The solution:**
- Set hard stop time (mine: 5 PM)
- Shut laptop and physically close it
- Put it in a drawer or different room
- "Out of sight, out of mind" is real

**What I learned the hard way:** If you can see your laptop, you'll "just check one thing." Close it. Hide it.

### Boundary 2: Communication Availability
**The problem:** People expect instant responses because you're "home anyway"

**The solution:**
- Set Slack/Teams status to your actual availability
- Turn off notifications outside work hours
- Communicate your boundaries: "I check messages 9-5, will respond next business day after that"
- **Stick to it**‚Äîeven when it feels uncomfortable

**Reality:** Bosses push back initially. Then they adjust. Your sanity is worth the awkward conversation.

### Boundary 3: Household Mixing
**The problem:** "Can you just do the laundry real quick?" / "Can you watch the kids for an hour?"

**The solution:**
- Set expectations with household members: work hours = I'm not home
- Get a door that closes (or visual barrier if no door)
- Explain: "I'm not available during work hours, same as if I was at an office downtown"

**Real talk:** This is hardest with kids. Noise-cancelling headphones help. Patience helps more.

## The Productivity Systems That Stuck

I've tried every productivity system. Here's what lasted:

### Time Blocking (Simple Version)
- **9-11 AM:** Deep Work Block 1 (most important task of the day)
- **11-12 PM:** Communication Block 1 (emails, Slack)
- **12-1 PM:** Lunch + break
- **1-3 PM:** Deep Work Block 2 (second most important task)
- **3-5 PM:** Communication Block 2 (meetings, emails, admin)

**Why it works:** Protects deep work time. Batches similar tasks. Prevents meeting overload.

### The "One Thing" Rule
Every morning, before opening email:  
**"What's the ONE thing that, if completed today, makes today successful?"**

Do that thing first. In the first deep work block. Before anything else.

**Days I follow this rule:** Productive, satisfying, feel accomplished  
**Days I don't:** Busy but unproductive, frustrated by end of day

### The Pomodoro Technique (Modified)
- 90 minutes focused work
- 10 minute break (leave desk, move body)
- Repeat

Classic Pomodoro is 25 min work / 5 min break. Too short for me. 90/10 hits the sweet spot‚Äîlong enough to get into flow, short enough to prevent burnout.

## The Mistakes I Made (So You Don't Have To)

**Mistake 1: Working from bed or couch**  
Seems cozy. Destroys your back and your sleep quality. Your brain associates bed with work. Now you can't sleep. Get a desk.

**Mistake 2: Skipping "the commute"**  
Rolling out of bed straight to work = no mental transition. Adding a 10-minute walk before/after work = game-changing psychological separation.

**Mistake 3: Buying cheap chair**  
I went through THREE cheap chairs ($80 each = $240 total) before buying a real one ($360). Should've just bought the good chair first.

**Mistake 4: No dedicated workspace**  
"I'll just work from wherever" means you're always half-working and never fully relaxing. Pick a spot. That's work. Everywhere else is not work.

**Mistake 5: Trying to work 8+ hours of deep focus**  
You don't have 8 hours of focus in you. Nobody does. Research shows 3-4 hours of genuine deep work per day is the realistic max. The rest is communication, admin, breaks. Plan accordingly.

## Bottom Line

Working from home can be amazing or miserable. The difference is setup and boundaries.

Total investment: $400-700 + some discipline
